Ada Lovelace (1815-1852)

Ada Lovelace was an English mathematician and writer who is known for her work on Charles Babbage's proposed mechanical general-purpose computer, the Analytical Engine. Lovelace is credited with writing the world's first computer program, which was intended to be processed by the Analytical Engine.

Grace Hopper (1906-1992)

Grace Hopper was an American computer scientist and navy admiral who invented one of the first compilers for a programming language, which allowed programmers to write code in a more English-like format that the computer could understand. Hopper also played a key role in the development of COBOL, one of the first high-level programming languages.

Dennis Ritchie (1941-2011)

Dennis Ritchie was an American computer scientist who co-created the C programming language and was one of the creators of the Unix operating system. C has become one of the most widely used programming languages in history, and Unix has influenced the development of many other operating systems, including Linux and macOS.
